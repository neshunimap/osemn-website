OSEMN Framework – Walmart Example (Demand Forecasting)

Problem Statement: Demand Forecasting and Inventory Optimization in Walmart Using the OSEMN Framework

  • Obtain: Walmart collects data from multiple sources such as point-of-sale (POS) data from stores, online purchase transactions, customer loyalty program data, weather data and holiday calendars, inventory and supplier data.
  • Scrub: The data is cleaned and prepared by removing duplicates transactions, fixing missing or incorrect prices and quantities, and standardizing product names and categories.
  • Explore: Walmart analyzes the data to find patterns by identifying peak sales periods (holidays, weekends), understanding regional buying trends, and visualizing sales trends using charts and dashboards.
  • Model: Walmart builds predictive models to forecast product demand for each store, predict stock-outs and overstock situations and improve supply chain efficiency using machine learning models.
  • Interpret: The results are used to make business decisions such as improve product availability for customers and increase revenue and customer satisfaction.

Why this is a real-life use case: Walmart is known for using big data analytics and predictive modeling to manage one of the world’s largest retail supply chains, making OSEMN a perfect framework to describe their data-driven decision-making.
